Product Requirements Document (PRD): Cyber Threat Hunter Pro1. Executive SummaryCyber Threat Hunter Pro is an advanced Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) and behavioral analytics platform. It addresses the "Detection Gap"—the space between standard signature-based detection and the actual speed of Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs). By unifying global intelligence (STIX/TAXII) with local behavioral mapping (MITRE ATT&CK), the platform empowers security teams to visualize the "Kill Chain" and intercept adversaries during the early stages of an intrusion.2. Problem StatementReactive vs. Proactive: Current security tools notify analysts after a compromise. There is no unified view to "hunt" for indicators of an ongoing, silent breach.The "Context" Vacuum: Standalone alerts (e.g., a single failed login) lack the context of threat intelligence (e.g., that IP is associated with APT28).Framework Fragmentation: Many organizations want to follow MITRE ATT&CK but lack the tooling to automatically map their specific logs to the matrix.3. Goals & ObjectivesConsolidated Intelligence: Centralize disparate threat feeds into a single, normalized database.Behavioral Correlation: Move beyond simple IoCs (IPs/Hashes) to TTPs (Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures).Visual Storytelling: Transform complex log data into a chronological "Attack Story" via the Cyber Kill Chain.Operational Efficiency: Automate the manual task of cross-referencing logs with threat reports.4. Target PersonasJunior SOC Analyst (Tier 1): Needs clear, color-coded dashboards to identify high-risk alerts quickly.Senior Threat Hunter (Tier 3): Needs raw data access and the ability to pivot between intelligence reports and system telemetry.CISO: Needs high-level heatmaps to understand organizational risk posture against specific global threat actors.5. Functional Requirements (Detailed)5.1 Intelligence Management (CTI)Automated Ingestion: Support for TAXII 2.1 collections and STIX 2.1 bundles.Confidence Scoring: Automatically calculate a "Trust Score" for feeds based on historical accuracy.IoC Aging: Automatically expire or "deprioritize" old indicators (e.g., IPs that haven't been seen in 30 days).5.2 Behavioral Mapping (MITRE ATT&CK)Real-time Mapping: Ingested logs (Process, Network, Registry) must be tagged with Technique IDs (e.g., T1055 for Process Injection).Gap Analysis: Visualize which parts of the MITRE matrix have no coverage based on the current log sources.Actor Profiling: Compare local detections against the known TTPs of specific APT groups (e.g., "Matches 80% of APT34 activity").5.3 Kill Chain Visualization & HuntingStage Mapping: Map events to Recon, Weaponization, Delivery, Exploitation, Installation, C2, and Actions on Objectives.Timeline Pivot: Interactive slider to view how an attack progressed over hours, days, or months.Graph Exploration: A node-link diagram showing the relationship between a Malicious File -> Process -> Network Connection.5.4 Reporting & IntegrationExecutive Summaries: One-page PDF overviews of the threat landscape.Technical IR Reports: Markdown/PDF exports containing full event timelines and forensic evidence.API Webhooks: Ability to push high-confidence alerts to external tools like Slack or Jira.6. Non-Functional RequirementsPerformance: Search queries across 1 million logs must return in < 2 seconds.Availability: 99.9% uptime for the ingestion engine.Scalability: Microservices architecture to allow independent scaling of the parser and the web UI.Compliance: Logging and data handling must align with SOC2/GDPR requirements for sensitive data.7. Risks & MitigationsRisk: Data Overload (Too many logs crashing the DB).Mitigation: Implement a "Hot/Cold" storage strategy using Redis for recent events and MongoDB for archives.Risk: High False Positives.Mitigation: Implement a multi-stage correlation logic where an alert is only fired if multiple TTPs are observed on a single host.8. Success MetricsMTTD (Mean Time to Detect): Target a 40% reduction in identifying multi-stage attacks.Analyst Fatigue: Measured by the ratio of "Actionable Alerts" vs. "Dismissed Alerts."Intelligence Utilization: Percentage of ingested IoCs that resulted in a successful detection.
